Mark Emmert Wiki

The current president of the National Collegiate Athletic Association is Mark Ellen Emmert. He is the NCAA's fifth CEO; he was chosen incoming president on April 27, 2010, and took office on November 1, 2010. Emmert was formerly the 30th president of his alma school, the University of Washington, entering office in June 2004, becoming the first alumni to lead UW in 48 years. He departed Washington on October 1, 2010, after announcing his intention to run for the NCAA presidency on April 27, 2010. In recognition of his contributions to the University of Washington, the Board of Regents named him President Emeritus.

Mark Emmert Early Life/Career

Early Life: - Emmert was born on December 16, 1952, in Fife, Washington, and graduated from Fife High School in 1971. He attended Green River Community College in Auburn, Washington, before transferring to the University of Washington in the spring of 1973, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1975. He went on to receive a Master of Public Administration in 1976 and a Ph.D. in 1983 from Syracuse University's Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs.

Professional Career: - At the University of Colorado, Boulder, Emmert held a number of academic administration posts. On his resume, he lists the following positions: Associate/Assistant Professor and Associate Dean responsible for daily management of academic and student affairs in the Graduate School of Public Affairs; Assistant to the UC System President and Associate Vice-Chancellor. Emmert was an American Council on Education Fellow.
