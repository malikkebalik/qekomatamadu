Career

Shires is an American singer-songwriter and fiddle player whose music blends elements of folk, country, and rock. She’s a highly respected figure in the Americana music scene, known for her powerful vocals, skilled instrumentation, and thoughtful songwriting.

Shires has also released seven solo albums, starting with “Being Here” in 2005 and most recently “Take It Like a Man” in 2022. Her solo work showcases her diverse musical influences and lyrical depth. She has collaborated with a wide range of artists, including her husband Jason Isbell, John Prine, Brandi Carlile, and Maren Morris.

Shires has won numerous awards and accolades, including a Grammy Award for Best Americana Album as part of Jason Isbell & the 400 Unit for their 2017 album “The Nashville Sound.”

Notable Travel, Events, and Experiences

As a solo artist and member of Jason Isbell & the 400 Unit, Shires has crisscrossed the globe, from intimate clubs to sprawling festival stages. These journeys exposed her to different cultures and perspectives, enriching her songwriting and performances.

Forming the supergroup The Highwomen in 2016 alongside Brandi Carlile, Maren Morris, and Natalie Hemby offered a platform for female voices and collaboration, empowering Shires and celebrating sisterhood.

In 2018, Shires bravely went public about her struggles with addiction and subsequent recovery. This experience added depth and vulnerability to her music, resonating with fans and inspiring others.

Social Impact: Philanthropic, Charitable, and Community Involvement

Shires is a vocal advocate for artists’ rights and fair compensation. She actively supports organizations like MusiCares and Sweet Relief Musicians Fund, which provide critical assistance to musicians in times of need.

She cares deeply about children’s well-being. She’s supported organizations like St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital and Save the Children, raising awareness and funds for crucial pediatric care and child welfare initiatives.
