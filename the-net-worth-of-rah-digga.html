Rashia Fisher – A Brief Biography

Born on December 12, 1972, Rashia Fisher, popularly known as Rah Digga, has made a name for herself as an American hip-hop artist, actress, and model. She has earned the title of “one of rap’s most prominent women MCs.”

Her net worth is estimated to be $500,000 as of early 2017, accumulated from her years in the music industry and her appearances in various movies and television shows.

Rah Digga’s Net Worth: $500,000

In her earlier years, Digga showcased her academic prowess, excelling as a student in a private school in Maryland, scoring 1300 on her SATs, and graduating as salutatorian. She eventually obtained a degree in electrical engineering from the New Jersey Institute of Technology.

Her love for rapping was instilled by inspirations like Queen Latifah and Rakim. This passion led her to join groups like Twice the Flavor and later Outsisaz. It was during a performance at the Lyricist Lounge in New York City that Q-Tip, a member of A Tribe Called Quest, recognized her talent and expressed interest in collaborating with her. This collaboration marked the beginning of her rap career and contributed to her net worth.

While initially planned to sign with Elektra Records, a shift occurred when Q-Tip introduced her to Busta Rhymes, leading her to become part of his group Flipmode Squad. This partnership resulted in numerous collaborations and her appearance in several of Busta Rhymes’ albums.

Following her time with Busta Rhymes’ group, she pursued a solo career and released her debut album “Dirty Harriet”, which became a chart-topper and established her as a prominent solo female artist. This success was followed by her participation in the album “Lyricist Lounge Vol. 1”, where she performed the track “Be Ok”.

Despite facing setbacks, such as her album “Everything is a Story” being leaked on the internet in 2004, Digga continued her musical journey. In 2010, she released a new album titled “Classic”. Her albums served to elevate her career and increase her net worth.

In addition to her music career, Digga ventured into acting, appearing in movies like “Da Hip Hop Witch”, “Thirteen Ghosts”, and MTV’s “Carmen: A Hip Hopera”. She also featured in documentaries such as “Queens of Hip Hop” (2003) and “Say My Name” (2009).

Currently, Digga remains active in the music industry, releasing songs via platforms like iTunes, Soundcloud, and Bandcamp.

On a personal note, Digga was previously married to Dewayne Battle, also known as Young Zee, and they have a daughter together named Sativa.
