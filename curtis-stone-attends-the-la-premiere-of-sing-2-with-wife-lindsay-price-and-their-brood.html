Curtis Stone attends the LA premiere of Sing 2 with wife Lindsay Price and their brood

Publish date: 2024-08-28

Curtis Stone is now based in Los Angeles with actress wife Lindsay Price and their brood, after having launched his first solo restaurant in Beverly Hills in 2014. 

And the Aussie celebrity chef, 46, attended the LA premiere of Illumination’s Sing 2 on Sunday with Lindsay, 45, and their sons Hudson, 10, and Emerson, seven. 

Curtis swapped his chef whites for a cosy Christmas sweater and warmly embraced his family as they posed for photos on the red carpet. 

A family affair! Curtis Stone, 46, swapped his chef whites for a Christmas sweater as he attended the Los Angeles premiere of Sing 2 on Sunday, with wife Lindsay Price, 45, and their sons Hudson, 10, and Emerson, seven (all pictured) 

The culinary expert teamed the sweater with a crisp pale blue dress shirt, black jeans and Nike sneakers.

He nestled in close to his actress wife Lindsay, who looked effortlessly chic in a beige sweater, light blue jeans, pink satin ballet flats and a matching pink coat.

Her brunette tresses cascaded past her shoulders in loose waves, and her makeup was elegant, enhancing her striking facial features. 

Curtis and Lindsay doted on their children as they posed for photos in front of the media wall, with the youngsters donning smart casual attire.

Sing 2, a computer-animated musical comedy, is the sequel to the highly successful 2016 film.  

The latest instalment sees a koala named Buster Moon (voiced by Matthew McConaughey) join his performers in preparing for their most dazzling stage show yet. 

However it’s not smooth sailing with Buster determined to get the world’s most reclusive rock star, lion rock legend Clay Calloway (voiced by Bono), to join them.

Also lending their voice to the film are other notable names including Reese Witherspoon, Scarlett Johansson, Bobby Cannavale, Pharrell Williams and Halsey.

Curtis has become a household name in both Australia and the United States, cashing in as a best-selling cookbook author and television personality.  

He is also the owner of two restaurants in Los Angeles, Gwen and Maude, named after his grandmothers.

Despite his fame, Curtis told The Australian Women’s Weekly in 2018 that his childhood in Melbourne was ‘humble’.

‘I didn’t grow up wealthy,’ he said. ‘We lived in a neighbourhood that was pretty humble, where I played a lot of football, and I feel more at home in that environment than I do anywhere. But it’s true that in LA we live a fabulous life.’
