The Disappearance of Jin Fang

The mysterious disappearance of Jin Fang has captured significant attention and raised many questions.

Introduction

Jin Fang, a Chinese woman from Guangdong, embarked on a journey to the United States, specifically Los Angeles, after months of online communication. Her story took a tragic turn when she vanished without a trace.

Arrival in Los Angeles

On July 14, 2023, Jin Fang arrived in Los Angeles, ready to meet John Root Fitzpatrick, a resident of Morongo Valley, California. After spending a night at a downtown hotel, she boarded a train to Palm Springs the following day.

Engagement with John Root Fitzpatrick

Fang had enlisted Fitzpatrick’s services as her tour guide during her stay in the United States. They had been in contact for six months before her arrival. Fitzpatrick was to introduce her to the Morongo Basin, and they planned a camping trip together. However, the exact duration of their trip remained undetermined.

Discovery of Fitzpatrick’s Death

In a tragic turn of events, Fitzpatrick was found deceased in Harper Canyon within the Anza-Borrego Desert State Park. His blue Toyota Tacoma, registered as 24478S1, was discovered nearby. The circumstances surrounding his death remain undisclosed.

Concerns about Jin’s Whereabouts

Following Fitzpatrick’s death, concerns grew about Jin Fang’s whereabouts. The last contact with her was on July 22, when she posted on social media about hiking in Joshua Tree National Park with Fitzpatrick. However, her phone stopped functioning three days later, and she has not been heard from since. The San Bernardino County police and Fang’s family have released a missing person’s bulletin, appealing for any information about her location.

An online fundraising initiative has been launched to gather a reward of US$10,000 for any valuable tips regarding Jin Fang’s location. It is still unclear what happened to her, and the search for answers continues.
