How To Style Permed Hair

Publish date: 2024-08-14

Styling permed hair can be an exciting journey filled with endless possibilities for creating stunning looks. Whether you prefer voluminous curls, sleek waves, or sophisticated updos, this guide offers practical tips and styling techniques how to style permed hair.

  • Invest in high-quality hair care products specifically designed for permed hair, including shampoos, conditioners, and styling creams or gels. Look for products that provide moisture, enhance curls, and protect against heat damage.
  • Celebrate the unique texture of your permed hair by embracing its natural curl pattern. Avoid excessive heat styling and harsh chemical treatments that can damage your curls and compromise their integrity.
  • Explore various curl sizes and techniques to create diverse looks that complement your style and personality. Experiment with spiral curls, loose waves, or tight ringlets to discover the perfect curl pattern for your hair.
  • Boost volume and definition in your permed hair by using volumizing mousses or foams. Apply the product to damp hair and scrunch your curls upward to encourage bounce and lift.
  • Preserve your permed curls overnight by sleeping on a satin or silk pillowcase to minimize friction and prevent frizz. Alternatively, wrap your hair in a silk scarf or use a satin bonnet to protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Minimize heat damage and maintain the health of your permed hair by incorporating heat-free styling methods into your routine. Experiment with twist-outs, bantu knots, or flexi rod sets to achieve beautiful curls without using heat.
  • Protect your permed hair from environmental damage and manipulation by wearing protective hairstyles such as braids, twists, or buns. These styles help retain moisture and prevent breakage, allowing your curls to thrive.
  • When using styling tools such as curling irons or flat irons, always apply a heat protectant spray to shield your permed hair from heat damage. Use low to medium heat settings and avoid excessive heat exposure to maintain the health of your curls.
  • Schedule regular trims every 8-12 weeks to remove split ends and maintain the shape of your permed hairstyle. Trimming your hair regularly helps prevent breakage and promotes healthy growth.
  • Experiment with versatile styling options for permed hair, including half-up hairstyles, braided updos, and chic ponytails. Get creative with accessories such as hair clips, headbands, or scarves to add flair to your look.
