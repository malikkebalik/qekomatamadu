Ahsoka Tano is one of the most popular characters in the Star Wars franchise, having appeared in various media, such as The Clone Wars, Star Wars Rebels, The Mandalorian, and Ahsoka. She is a former Jedi Padawan of Anakin Skywalker and a Rebel Alliance spy who fought against the Galactic Empire. But what happened to her after the events of Star Wars Rebels? Is she still alive, or did she meet her end in some unknown way? This article will explore the fate of Ahsoka Tano and the clues that hint at her possible survival.

Ahsoka’s Journey

Ahsoka Tano was discovered on her homeworld of Shili by Jedi Master Plo Koon, who brought her to the Jedi Temple on Coruscant. There, she was assigned as the Padawan of Anakin Skywalker, who nicknamed her “Snips” for her snippy attitude. Together, they fought in many battles during the Clone Wars, the conflict between the Galactic Republic and the Confederacy of Independent Systems. Ahsoka proved to be a brave and loyal apprentice, as well as a skilled and resourceful fighter.

However, Ahsoka’s faith in the Jedi Order was shattered when she was framed for a bombing at the Jedi Temple and expelled from the Order. Although she was eventually cleared of the charges, she refused to rejoin the Jedi, feeling betrayed and disillusioned by their actions. She left the Order and went into hiding, adopting a new identity as “Fulcrum”, a secret informant for the growing rebellion against the Empire.

Ahsoka later reunited with her former master, who had fallen to the dark side and become Darth Vader, the Sith apprentice of Emperor Palpatine. She confronted him on the Sith world of Malachor, where they engaged in a fierce lightsaber duel. The outcome of the duel was unknown, as the Sith temple they were in collapsed and exploded. Ahsoka was last seen walking into the temple’s depths, while Vader limped away from the rubble.

Ahsoka’s Survival

Many fans wondered if Ahsoka had survived her encounter with Vader, or if she had perished in the explosion. The answer was revealed in the fourth and final season of Star Wars Rebels, where Ahsoka made a surprising return. It was shown that she was saved from certain death by Ezra Bridger, a young Jedi who used a mysterious portal to travel back in time and pull her out of the temple before it exploded. Ezra then returned to his own time, leaving Ahsoka in the past.

Ahsoka then waited for the right moment to leave Malachor, and eventually made contact with the Rebel Alliance again. She helped them in their fight against the Empire, and was present at the Battle of Endor, where the second Death Star was destroyed. She then joined Sabine Wren, a Mandalorian warrior and friend of Ezra, in a quest to find Ezra, who had disappeared along with the Imperial Grand Admiral Thrawn during a space battle.

Ahsoka’s appearance in Star Wars Rebels confirmed that she was alive and well, at least until the end of the original trilogy. However, her fate after that remained unknown, until she appeared again in the second season of The Mandalorian, a live-action series set five years after the events of Return of the Jedi. She was portrayed by Rosario Dawson, who brought the animated character to life on the screen.

In The Mandalorian, Ahsoka was seen on the forest planet of Corvus, where she was trying to liberate the people from the tyranny of a local magistrate. She encountered Din Djarin, the titular Mandalorian, who was looking for a Jedi to train his adopted son, Grogu, a Force-sensitive child of Yoda’s species. Ahsoka sensed Grogu’s connection to the Force, but refused to train him, fearing that he would fall to the dark side like Anakin did. She instead told Din to take Grogu to the planet of Tython, where he could reach out to other Jedi through the Force.

Ahsoka also revealed that she was looking for Thrawn, implying that she had not found him or Ezra yet. She asked the magistrate where Thrawn was, but did not get an answer. She then left Corvus, presumably to continue her search.

Ahsoka’s Future

Ahsoka’s appearance in The Mandalorian was a huge surprise for the fans, and also a teaser for her own spin-off series, Ahsoka, which was announced by Disney in 2023. The series will follow Ahsoka’s adventures after the events of The Mandalorian, and will likely explore her quest to find Thrawn and Ezra, as well as her role in the galaxy after the fall of the Empire. The series will also feature David Tennant as the voice of Morai, a convor bird that is connected to Ahsoka and the Force.

Ahsoka’s future is still shrouded in mystery, but one thing is certain: she is not dead. She is alive and kicking, and ready to face new challenges and enemies. She is one of the most beloved and iconic characters in the Star Wars universe, and her story is far from over. She is Ahsoka Tano, and she is a legend.
