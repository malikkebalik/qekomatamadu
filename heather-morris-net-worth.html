Heather Morrison Wiki Biography

Heather Elizabeth Morris was born on 1st February 1987, in Thousand Oaks, California USA, and is an actress, model and singer, probably best known to the world as Brittany Pierce in the TV series “Glee” (2009-2015), and for appearing as Bess in the comedy film “Spring Breakers” (2012), among other roles. Her career has been active since 2006.

Although born in Thousand Oaks, California, Heather grew up in Scottsdale, Arizona, and went to Desert Mountain High School. After matriculation, she enrolled at the local university, but then decided that it would be better for her to pursue her career, and moved to Los Angeles. She had begun taking dancing lessons when she was only nine years old, and as she got older she became more focused on dancing. She would enter numerous competitive events while she was young, and just continued into teen and then adult life. In 2006 she appeared on the TV “So You Think You Can Dance” show, but fell out of the competition before the final 20. Nevertheless, she continued to pursue her dream, and joined Beyoncé the next year on her tour The Beyoncé Experience as a back-up dancer, appearing in such shows as “Saturday Night Live”, “The Ellen DeGeneres Show”, “Total Request Live”, and “American Music Awards”, so establishing her net worth.

In 2008 her acting career began, when she was featured as a dancer in the TV series “Swingtown”, and just a year later she had her big screen debut in the comedy “Fired Up!”. Also in 2009, she was selected for the role of Brittany S. Pierce in the popular TV musical-comedy series “Glee”, and through 2015 appeared in 95 episodes, which only increased her net worth by a large margin, while also making her extremely popular. She stayed quite focused on the show, as she made only a few other appearances in short films, such as “The Elevator” (2011) and “A Sense of Humor” (2011), but also made an appearance in her second feature film “Post” (2011), next to Scott Haze and Katherine Boecher. She then continued with film roles in such productions as “Spring Breakers” (2012), while also lending her voice to Katie in the animated film “Ice Age: Continental Drift” (2012), all of which added to her net worth. Her next role was in the film “Romantically Speaking” (2015), as Ariel, and then as Gaby in the film “Most Likely to Die”, the same year.

Most recently, Heather appeared in the TV series “Go-Go Boy Interrupted” (2016), and in the film “Folk Hero & Funny Guy” (2016), which also improved her wealth.
