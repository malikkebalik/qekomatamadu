What is Debbie Gibson’s net worth

Debbie Gibson, the American singer, songwriter, actress, music producer, and reality television competition participant, has a net worth of $2 million dollars. She rose to fame as an international teen starlet in the 1980s.

Early Life

Debbie Gibson, born in Brooklyn, New York on August 31, 1970, grew up in suburban Merrick, New York, on Long Island. She was the third of Diane and Joseph Gibson’s four daughters.

During her childhood, Debbie studied piano under acclaimed classical pianist Morton Estrin.

Debbie Gibson’s Music Career

Debbie Gibson began writing and producing her own music as a teenager, and her demo tape landed her a development deal with Atlantic Records. Her debut single “Only in My Dreams” landed on the Billboard Hot 100 and helped her secure a recording contract. Her debut album “Out of the Blue” was an instant success, with four singles reaching the top five of the Hot 100 Chart. She became the youngest artist to write, produce, and perform a No. 1 single on the Billboard Hot 100 with “Foolish Beat.”

Gibson released her second album “Electric Youth” in 1989, which spent five weeks at No. 1 on the Top 200 Album chart. Her single “Lost in Your Eyes” was No. 1 on the Hot 100 for three weeks. She recorded two more albums for Atlantic Records before signing with EMI’s SBK Records and releasing “Think With Your Heart.” She later formed her own record label, Espiritu, and released her sixth and seventh albums “Deborah” and “Golden Egg” in 1997 and 2001, respectively.

Throughout her career, Gibson has released nine studio albums and had five top ten singles including “Foolish Beat,” “Only in My Dreams,” “Shake Your Love,” “Out of the Blue,” and “Lost in Your Eyes.”

Debbie Gibson’s Career in Theatre and Film

Debbie Gibson transitioned from teenage pop star to successful theatre and film actress. She made her Broadway debut in 1992 as Eponine in “Les Miserables” and went on to star as Sandy in “Grease” in London’s West End. Gibson has also appeared in “Funny Girl,” “Beauty and the Beast,” “Gypsy,” and many other productions.

On screen, she has starred in “Mega Shark Versus Giant Octopus,” “Mega Python vs. Gatoroid,” and “Mega Shark Versus Mecha Shark,” as well as the UP TV movie “The Music in Me” alongside Gloria Reuben. Gibson has also appeared on reality shows such as “The Celebrity Apprentice” and “Dancing with the Stars,” and served as a judge on Nickelodeon’s “America’s Most Musical Family.”

Stalker Incidents, Personal Life, and Health Struggles

Debbie Gibson has faced several stalkers throughout her career. Michael Falkner, a fan from Wisconsin, was arrested outside a theater where Gibson was performing in 1998. She had received threatening calls, letters, emails, and faxes from him.

Gibson was engaged to Jonathan Kanterman and has been in a long-term relationship with Rutledge Taylor since 2008.

In early 2013, Gibson was diagnosed with Lyme Disease and has been battling it ever since.

After the death of singer Prince, Gibson spoke out about drug abuse in her past. She admitted to abusing Tylenol PM and Xanax in her twenties while touring with theater companies.

Debbie Gibson’s Struggles with Home Ownership

In 2005, Debbie Gibson purchased a home in Los Angeles for $1.275 million. However, in 2009, she reportedly fell into default on her mortgage payments and almost lost the property to foreclosure. As a result, she sold the house in July of that year for $900,000.
