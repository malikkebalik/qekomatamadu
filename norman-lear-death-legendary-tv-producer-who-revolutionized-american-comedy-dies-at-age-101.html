The show became an immediate hit, resonating with viewers from all political backgrounds.

Lear’s ability to infuse serious topics into the standard domestic comedy formula set him apart. Not content with conventional storytelling, Lear also ventured into meta territory with shows like “Mary Hartman, Mary Hartman” and its spinoff “Fernwood 2 Night.” These shows parodied daytime soaps and talk shows, offering a satirical take on the absurdities of everyday life.
